Importance of randomness in Computer Science

Without us realizing it, our devices, whether they are mobile phones, computers, tablets, etc..., are constantly generating countless "random" numbers. Even though we may not be aware of this, these numbers are crucial for our security, mostly used in cryptography in the form of nonces. A nonce (Number used once) is a random number that can only be used once in a protocol, ensuring that a specific message is unique and that another with the same content is not accepted. Among many other uses, some notable ones include:

  • Authentication: Nonces are used by authentication protocols to ensure that old communications cannot be replayed.
  • Initialization Vectors: Alongside a secret key, an initialization vector uses a nonce to encrypt data. Adding this random number to encryption prevents repetitive patterns that could allow attackers to decrypt the message.
  • Identification In logins or account recovery, two-factor authentication uses random numbers.
  • Secure Connection: The TLS protocol uses 2 nonces, one from the client and one from the server. We constantly use this protocol to browse the internet, providing confidentiality and authenticity of data during transmission. As we can imagine, it is important that these values are as random as possible, as predictability would compromise security.
